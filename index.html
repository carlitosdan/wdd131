About Lima, Perú

Lima, the capital of Peru, is a vibrant city that blends history, culture, and modern life. Located on the Pacific coast, it is the country’s largest city and economic hub, home to nearly 10 million people.

Founded in 1535 by the Spanish conquistador Francisco Pizarro, Lima was once the most important city in Spanish South America. Its historic center, a UNESCO World Heritage site, is filled with colonial-era architecture, including the Plaza Mayor, Lima Cathedral, and San Francisco Monastery, known for its underground catacombs.

A Culinary Capital

Lima is often called the gastronomic capital of Latin America, offering some of the world’s best cuisine. Visitors can enjoy ceviche, Peru’s national dish, along with lomo saltado, anticuchos, and causa. The city is home to three of the top 5 restaurants in the world, according to The World's 50 Best Restaurants.
